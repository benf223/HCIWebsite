Introduction

Computers are always equipped with a mouse to allow us to to interact with the device. Even though we use computer shortcuts and the trackpad in our laptop the mouse always serves a essential purpose in completing tasks. As university students we use the regular mouse almost everyday. We have culturally mapped to the regular optical mouse as we have grown up our lives with it. Although, the regular mouse serves the purpose to navigate and interact with our computer the ergonomic mouse is proven to be capable to do tasks that a regular mouse does.

In recent years, the ergonomic mouse has been rising in demand for several purposes for its functionalities, design, health purposes and comfort to the hand. The focus of this research is to find out out if users will prefer to use a ergonomic mouse than a normal mouse for completing tasks on a computer.

We are focusing on the preferences of the user when using an ergonomic mouse to research what the user's perspective on the ergonomic mouse to be able to perform a task.

Developing the Hypothesis

Our interactive device for our HCI assignment 2 is the 3M ergonomic mouse, that we will investigate as a team. Prior to research we developed a hypothesis that we wanted to test:

We hypothesise that the design of the ergonomic mouse will cause less strain on the wrist because of the arm muscle placement and position

Initially we considered experimenting with measuring the stress on the arm and comparing the ergonomic mouse and a regular mouse. We thought about looking into both the comfort and discomfort and muscle placement position while using a mouse. It is proven that a regular mouse to have given arm fatigue, wrist and even carpal tunnel syndrome to users in the long term. However due to limited available resources we couldn't obtain the equipment to measure these factors within the time frame given for our experiment.

To mitigate this we developed another hypothesis: We hypothesise that users will prefer to use a ergonomic mouse rather than a normal mouse for completing tasks on a computer.

That's why we have chosen to study the 3M ergonomic mouse.

We have decided to experiment with the participants and then to undertake a questionnaire. Each task we have asked participants to do involves movement, clicking and positioning of the ergonomic mouse. These factors should provide the participant with enough information to make informed opinions of the ergonomic mouse. This will hopefully prove our theory that users will prefer to use a ergonomic mouse rather than a normal mouse for completing tasks on a computer.
