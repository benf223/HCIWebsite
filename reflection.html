Jethro

This was a different experience for me as I have never conducted an HCI experiment before, however it reminded me of my physics experiments back in high school because of all the different types of variable in the experiment (independent, dependant, constant). From my point of view, I struggled a little bit with the experiment because I found that as a team we couldn't think of many tasks for the user to do on the ergonomic mouse. This led to a delay in starting our experiments which slowed down the team production/output. However, once we had the data, I found that we analysed it fast and managed to get some useful data. This fast analysis was due to our team having high cohesion with each other. As a team I felt that our strong points were the fact that we didn't argue with each other and that we respected each others ideas, even though we thought that that idea was wrong, we listened and talked about it as a team. Due to that I strongly feel that I would gladly work with this team for other group projects. I also struggled with time management because I had many things due like the R&D project, Web Development Assignment 2 and Software Engineering. This made it difficult to balance the work I needed to do for this assignment.

During the experiment my role was to guide the participants into the room, explain to them what was going to happen and to guide them through each task. I found this to be an enjoyable experience but also difficult because I had to act in the same way and talk the same way to keep the experiment "the same" for every participant. I also did some research into how to run an HCI experiment and tried to plan our experiment based on those findings. These findings helped the team structure the experiment well and to ask the right questions to get the data that I needed

Ben

I have learnt much from working on this assignment. While I initially felt interested in the paper, as we went through the paper my enjoyment of it decreased. While being aware of how people interact with technology is important, I do not believe that I could do this type of assignment again. This assignment while I did participate and provide relevant input I believe that we had much to improve on. I did not take an active role during the experiments I spent that time recording the participants responses to questions, I felt that I could not spend time doing the research but instead being aware of it from the perspective of a developer which is more relevant to my Computer Science and Software Development majors.

I was able to help my team to finalise parts of our assignment while providing valuable feedback to each member so that they can complete their work to the best of their ability. I did also find myself critiquing most of how we did this assignment as we often found ourselves changing how we were experimenting or presenting the data.

I found this assignment also quite confusing in the sense that we could not as a group determine what type of research we were doing. I know I was not alone in my confusion as most of my group did not understand what our methodology was and I think that our research took longer to be finished due to this uncertainty. I also think that due to this as a group we did not perform our experiments very consistently thereby having a negative impact on our results. I am sure that with further learning on my behalf I could have assisted my team in better performing during the assignment.
