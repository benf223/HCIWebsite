Study Design

Variables

  • view_listTable of Variables
    Variable Properties Description
    Point time Time taken to point to and click target.
    Point displacement Distance from original cursor location to target location. Implicitly randomised due to randomised positions of targets.
    Target radius Distance from centre to edge of target circles.
    Target colour Ensure high contrast and easy identification of target, and to eliminate discrimination in the case of participant colour blindness.
    Value: Black (#000000)
    Device used Maintained to eliminate variation in participant experience.
    Value: ASUS Transformer Book Flip TP300LA
    Mouse used Maintained to eliminate variation in participant experience.
    Value: IBM laser mouse
    Setting The experiment was conducted at AUT in the R&D lab room located on floor 5 of the WT tower.
    Screen pitch/angle Will be kept at a set angle to keep the test environment as consistent as possible.
    Value: 90°
    Reason: Both close to ideal viewing angle for the average user and easy to measure.
    Screen size Maintained to eliminate variation in participant experience. Borderless fullscreen will be used at all times.
    Value: 1366x768
    Screen brightness Maintained to eliminate variation in participant experience.
    Value: 100%
    Number of targets objects to be clicked Maintained to eliminate variation in iterations participants have to learn and upskill in during the test.
    Value: 20
    Viewing angle People’s height and posture affect eye level and inherently, viewing angle. This could affect the clarity or distortion of display.
    Reaction time Because times are recorded from the participants’ previous click, there will be a short but potentially inconsistent time included for the location and reaction to locating the target.
