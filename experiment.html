Hypothesis

We hypothesise that users will prefer to use a ergonomic mouse rather than a normal mouse for completing tasks on a computer.

Tasks for participant to complete

  • clear_allList of tasks
    1. Spend time getting used to the mouse.
    2. Simple connect the dots
    3. More difficult connect the dots
    4. Drawing a picture with paint
    5. Selecting an moving files between folders
    6. Scrolling down a web page

Execution

  • clear_allList of questions posed to participant
    1. What are your initial impressions of the design ergonomic design?
    2. How often do you use a regular mouse?
    3. How long do you think it would take to get used to this mouse?
    4. For each task how confident did you feel accomplishing it on a scale of 1 to 10.
    5. On a scale from 1 to 10 how confident are you using the ergonomic mouse?
    6. Does it feel more comfortable than the regular mouse you normally use?
    7. Do you normally experience any form of pain while using regular mouse?
    8. Did you experience any pain/strain using this mouse?
    9. Which task was hardest to do?
    10. Which task was easiest to do?
    11. How does the thumb button change how you click?
    12. Do you prefer to left and right click with one thumb on the ergo mouse or two fingers on a normal mouse?
    13. How long do you think you could use this mouse consecutively?
    14. Do you think this mouse is usable in other scenarios?
    15. What do you think about the scroll button?
    16. Do you think university students would like to use a mouse like this?
    17. Would you use the ergo mouse again?
    18. Would you consider purchasing this mouse?
